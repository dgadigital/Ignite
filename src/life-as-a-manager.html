What Does a Business Manager Do?

The role is designed to lead, build, engage, and motivate teams to achieve great outcomes. The role requires the incumbent to coach, mentor and develop team members, create a culture of high performance, and an environment where open and authentic conversations regularly occur. Business Manager's are given financial accountability for defined markets segments and are required to meet and exceed sales targets. The role requires the person to create and drive sales strategies to suit the market, and empower team members to be accountable for their results.

Creating opportunities

  • Anticipates consequences in decision-making, including their impact on service delivery and function.
  • Anticipates, plans, and manages change within own team. Works to drive structural, team and functional efficiencies as they emerge.
  • Acts to resolve client queries and stakeholder concerns and maintains relationships for satisfactory commercial outcomes.
  • Drives internal collaboration and process efficiencies to execute client outcomes.

Strong relationships see results.

Creating an authentic and passionate culture with open dialogue, enabling people to be accountable for their work, whilst supporting career development, and recognises and celebrates success.

Managers help set challenging objectives and measures for all team members, hold continuous performance conversations with your team members to provide feedback, recognise successes, and improve performance where required.
